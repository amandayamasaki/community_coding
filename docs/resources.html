About North Lawndale

North Lawndale is located on the westside of Chicago. The predominantly black neighborhood contains 35,276 people (2015). In 1890, the area was heavily populated by Bohemian and Czech immigrants who lived in the popular architecture of greystone buildings. By the 1950s, blacks resided in North Lawndale after coming from the southside of Chicago and southern states. In 1966, Dr. Martin Luther King Jr. lived at 1550 S. Hamlin Ave while campaigning against housing discrimination, which was once common in Chicago. After King’s death, there were many riots in the 60s followed by high unemployment and crime rates. North Lawndale is home to the original Sears tower, now called the Nichols Tower, and the Homan Square Community Center.

North Lawndale is known for different churches with different religions. The schools around North Lawndale consists of both public and private. North Lawndale is known for violence and greystone structure. There is more to this community. This community is a place where some people grew up at, so they don’t really see anything wrong here.

North Lawndale gives you a lot of opportunities. The people here try to help one another. They would like to see improvement in this community. For example, at the Homan Square Community Center, they have a basketball court,pool, and also a clinic for people to be healthy and safe. If you walk over to the YMCA, on the glass windows, they have papers that will help people have the opportunity to have a job.
