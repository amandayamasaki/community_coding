#LawndaleCoding

Our Program
This program is called Community Coding by the School of the Art Institute of Chicago held in Homan Square (North Lawndale
Community). In these 2 weeks, we learned the basics of creating websites by practicing with HTML and CSS. We also had many
discussions on how technology impacts and influences the community. Our goal is to use technology to change how the rest of Chicago
views the West Side. We wanted to create a website for the community where people can have access to events, resources, and information about what goes on in North Lawndale. We hope to give the community a voice to show that there are many positive things and wonderful opportunities not just in North Lawndale, but the whole West Side.
T.R.A.C.E ( Teens Reimagining Art, Community, and Environment)
Instagram: @teensreimaginin

T.R.A.C.E is a program run by the Chicago Park District. They meet in Hamilton Park on the South Side of Chicago in Englewood.
This organization brings South Side teens together as creative artists and educators. These “TRACErs” collaborate with the community
to design and compose art. To celebrate their 10th anniversary, they have designed a mural on 58th and Halsted. This is to celebrate the
work they’ve accomplished over the past decade, and also recognize their supporters and celebrate all past, present, and future Tracers.
Lawndale Coding collaborated with them by making a QR code to send the viewers to information about the figures in their mural.
